The Clinksy landing page should feel modern, editorial, and calm — Bang & Olufsen-inspired aesthetic, not Lovable-style AI generative flex.

**Why:** The audience is anxious first-time UK home buyers, not AI early adopters. Lovable-style heroes (giant prompt box generating the page) make them feel like they're in someone else's product. A B&O-style monochrome editorial layout signals "considered and premium" without ever shouting "AI." The AI chat should be the hero *element* (front and centre, large, looking like a real friendly conversation) — but never the hero *gimmick*.

**How to apply:**
- Palette: ink near-black (#0A0A0A) + bone warm off-white (#FAFAF7) + neutral grays. No cream/terracotta. One restrained accent at most.
- CTAs: B&O move — transparent with thin black border, fill to solid black with white text on hover. Primary CTAs use solid black with subtle hover shift.
- Hero: small wordmark, mid-sized serif headline, the chat panel is the visual centrepiece (large, on-screen, mid-conversation), a single CTA. No door illustration or other decorative SVG.
- Typography: Fraunces serif headlines (still warm even in mono) + Inter body. Editorial rhythm — restrained sizes (H1 ~56–64px, H2 ~36–44px), tight tracking on display sizes.
- Section design: lots of whitespace. Drop alternating section backgrounds. Borderless or near-borderless cards. Thin hairline dividers only. Card-heavy layouts should be avoided.
- Reference: Apple Intelligence page, Notion AI page, Linear marketing site, B&O product pages — all keep AI/product front and centre without performing it.
