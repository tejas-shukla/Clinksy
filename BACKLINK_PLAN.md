# Clinkeys — Backlink & Authority Playbook

The on-page/technical SEO is done (schema, sitemap, llms.txt, AI-crawler access,
answer-first content). This is the off-site work that actually lifts rankings and
gets Clinkeys cited by Google and AI engines. Off-site authority is ~50% of the
ranking battle for a new domain — it can't be automated, but this playbook makes
it systematic.

**North-star metric:** number of referring domains (unique sites linking to you),
tracked monthly. Aim for 5–10 quality links in the first 90 days.

---

## 1. Foundation (do these first — quick, high-trust links)

These are easy "citations" that establish legitimacy. Do them in week 1.

- [ ] **Google Business Profile** (if you have any UK address) — free, strong trust signal.
- [ ] **Crunchbase** company profile → links back to clinkeys.com.
- [ ] **LinkedIn Company Page** for Clinkeys → add website.
- [ ] **X / Twitter, Instagram, Facebook page** — link in bio; social profiles get crawled.
- [ ] **Product directories**: Product Hunt (launch), BetaList, SaaSHub, AlternativeTo.
- [ ] **UK startup directories**: UK Startup Directory, Startups.co.uk listing.
- [ ] **Free web directories** (only reputable ones): Free Index UK, Cylex UK.

Expected: ~8–12 links, low authority individually but they build a baseline.

---

## 2. Community presence (where AI engines actually source answers)

ChatGPT/Perplexity heavily cite Reddit, forums, and Q&A sites. Be genuinely
helpful — link only when it truly answers the question (spammy links get removed
and hurt you).

- **Reddit**: r/HousingUK, r/UKPersonalFinance, r/FirstTimeBuyer, r/UKmortgages.
  Answer real questions in depth; link a specific guide when it's the best answer.
- **MoneySavingExpert forum** (the UK's biggest money forum) — mortgages & house-buying boards.
- **Quora**: answer "UK first-time buyer" / "stamp duty" / "conveyancing" questions.
- **MoneyHelper community / Citizens Advice forums** where relevant.

Rule of thumb: 9 genuinely helpful posts for every 1 that includes a link.

---

## 3. Guest posts & digital PR (the real authority drivers)

Target UK sites your audience already reads. Pitch a genuinely useful, original
article (not a thinly veiled ad).

**Tier 1 — property & money media (hard but huge):**
Metro Money, The Times Money Mentor, Which?, HomeOwners Alliance, Ideal Home,
Zoopla/Rightmove blogs, Mojo Mortgages blog, Habito blog.

**Tier 2 — realistic for a new brand:**
Personal-finance blogs (Be Clever With Your Cash, MoneyNest, PropertyRoad,
The Money Whisperer), regional property blogs, first-time-buyer YouTubers'/
creators' sites, mortgage-broker and conveyancer local blogs.

**Angles that get accepted (data + story beat opinion):**
- "First-time buyer mistakes that cost the most" (original checklist).
- "The true cost of buying a home in the UK in 2026" (link your cost guide as the data source).
- A small original survey/analysis (even 100 respondents via a poll) — journalists link to original data.
- Regional angle: "Cheapest places for first-time buyers in [region]".

---

## 4. Be the source AI engines cite (GEO)

- Publish **original data / definitive guides** (your deep-dives already qualify) — these are what get quoted.
- Get a **Wikipedia-adjacent footprint**: cited in listicles, "best first-time buyer resources" roundups. Search Google for `"first time buyer" "useful resources"` and `"first time buyer guide" intitle:resources` and pitch to be added.
- Keep the answer-first format (done) — concise, quotable answers rank in AI Overviews.
- **HARO / Featured / Qwoted / ResponseSource**: answer journalist requests on property/mortgages; earns high-authority media links + brand mentions LLMs pick up.

---

## 5. Partnerships (fits the Clinkeys model)

Since Clinkeys will match buyers with advisers/solicitors/surveyors, those pros
are natural link partners:

- Offer local mortgage brokers / conveyancers a **"recommended partner" or resource link exchange** (they link to your guides, you feature them when matching launches).
- Co-write a guide with a broker (they share it to their audience → link + reach).
- Get listed on professional bodies' member resource pages where possible.

## Tracking

Keep a simple sheet (or reuse your signups sheet in a new tab): Site | Type |
Contact | Date reached out | Status | Link live? Check referring domains monthly
in Google Search Console (Links report) — it shows who's linking to you.

## What NOT to do

- Don't buy links or use "1,000 backlinks for £5" services — Google penalties.
- Don't mass-spam forums with links — removed + reputational damage.
- Don't do reciprocal-link schemes at scale. A few genuine partners are fine.
- Quality over quantity: one link from HomeOwners Alliance beats 100 directory links.
