The Clinksy landing page must keep things simple and reassuring. Don't list every step or expose all the complexity of buying a home on the marketing surface — it makes first-time buyers anxious, which is the exact problem we're trying to solve.

**Why:** First-time buyers are already overwhelmed. The whole pitch is "buying is doable, with a calm guide." If the landing page itself looks like a wall of jargon and steps, we've already failed the promise.

**How to apply:**
- On the landing page, group the 10 detailed stages into 3-4 friendly phases (e.g. "Get ready → Find your home → Make it official → Get the keys") with very short labels.
- Reserve full step-by-step depth for the AI chat (where it's surfaced one step at a time, on demand) and for SEO long-form guide pages (e.g. /guides/mortgage-in-principle).
- Tone: confident and warm, not catastrophising. Acknowledge the confusion gently, then immediately show the calm.
- Avoid copy that piles on stress ("did you know you need to think about X, Y, Z, W..."). Avoid finance-bro confidence too. Aim closer to a thoughtful older sibling who's done it before.
